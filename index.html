Introduction

Noise pollution affects many of us, with an estimated 17 million people across Europe suffering chronic annoyance due to environmental noise (European Environment Agency, 2025). Beyond being a nuisance, noise pollution has serious health implications, and it is considered the third most significant environmental cause of ill health in Western Europe, resulting in the loss of approximately 1.3 million healthy life years annually (European Environment Agency, 2025). Conversely, nature is widely accepted as beneficial for both mental and physical health. (World Health Organization (no date) ). By 2050, two thirds of the world’s population are expected to live in urban areas, (United Nations, 2018) and so creating sustainable cities is essential.
Green walls are a way of introducing nature into urban environments, utilising the large areas of wall space typically available in modern cities. Both practical and aesthetic, green walls have many documented benefits, notably reducing urban heat and air pollution. ((Cardinali Balderrama Arztmann and Pottgiesser, 2023)). This article will firstly introduce the types of green walls. It will then focus on their application in reducing noise pollution, discussing both the direct and indirect effects they can have.
